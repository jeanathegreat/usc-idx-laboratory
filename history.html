IDX Laboratory History

Shaping the Future with Accenture’s Ideas Exchange Center (IDX)

Written by: Engr. Van B. Patiluna
Monday, 15 September 2014 20:08

The University of San Carlos, through the Department of Computer Engineering and Accenture Philippines, inaugurated the Accenture Ideas Exchange Center last August 28, 2014. The center, which is located at Room LB281TC, is the result of the academic partnership between USC and Accenture aimed at creating and exchanging ideas for technology development.

The affair was attended by Dr. Evelyn Taboada, Dean of the College of Engineering; Engr. Van Patiluna, Chair of the Department of Computer Engineering; Ms. Ambe Tierro, Accenture Global Delivery Network for Technology in the Philippines Lead; and Mr. Anton Guidotti, Assistant Director of Operations and the Cebu Operations Lead; with the special appearance of Fr. Louie Punzalan, SVD, Presidential Assistant for External Affairs.

Also gracing the event are the Accenture staff headed by Mr. Christian Quijano and Ms. Gladys Munsalud, department heads from the College of Engineering, faculty members, students and staff.

The Ideas Exchange Center (IDX) is a state of the art facility equipped with a Promethean 78” interactive whiteboard, Samsung 51” plasma HDTV, wireless high-definition multimedia streaming via Apple TV and a hi-fidelity public address sound system. It has an unusual layout with six (6) circular tables that sits four (4) for a total capacity of twenty-four (24). Each table has four (4) HP Pro One G400 All-In- One (AIO) personal computers, which give the center a clean and uncluttered look. It has a signature look with a huge Accenture poster on the inside and an embossed signage at the exterior.

IDX caters to classes which require usage as well as trainings and workshops. USC and Accenture look towards a long-term partnership by bringing their technology to the campus and training students and faculty members alike through this one-of-a-kind-facility. The keys to the center and a commemorative plaque were formally turned over to Dr. Taboada and Engr. Patiluna by Accenture headed by Ms. Tierro.
